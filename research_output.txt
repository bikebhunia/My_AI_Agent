PARIS: A COMPREHENSIVE OVERVIEW

BASIC INFORMATION:
Paris is the capital and largest city of France. Located on the river Seine in the centre of the Île-de-France region, Paris stands as the largest metropolitan area and fourth-most populous city in the European Union (EU).

POPULATION STATISTICS:
- City population: 2.04 million (as of January 2026)
- City area: 105.4 km² (40.7 sq mi)
- Metropolitan population: 13.2 million (as of January 2026)

NICKNAME AND SIGNIFICANCE:
Paris is nicknamed the "City of Light" (Ville-Lumière), partly because of its significant role in the Age of Enlightenment. This name reflects the city's historical importance as a center of intellectual and cultural advancement.

GEOGRAPHY:
The city is situated on the river Seine and forms the center of the Île-de-France region, making it a major geographical and cultural hub in Europe.

HISTORICAL LANDMARKS:
Notable historic sites include the Place des Vosges, a beautiful square dating back to the 1600s and one of the popular places to visit in Paris. The city contains numerous other architectural and cultural treasures that reflect its rich history.

CULTURE:
Paris is renowned for its culture, arts, entertainment, and historical significance. The city has played a crucial role in European history and continues to be a major center for art, fashion, cuisine, and intellectual thought.
